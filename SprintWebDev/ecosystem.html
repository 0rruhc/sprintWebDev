Biodiversity

The Amazon Rainforest is home to an estimated 10% of the world's known species, making it one of the most biodiverse regions on Earth. It hosts over 400 billion trees belonging to around 16,000 species, as well as countless mammals, birds, amphibians, and reptiles.

Layers of the Rainforest

The rainforest is divided into four layers:

  • the emergent layer
  • canopy
  • understory
  • forest floor

Each layer supports a unique ecosystem with specialized plant and animal species.

Threats and Conservation

Deforestation, illegal logging, and climate change pose serious threats to the Amazon ecosystem. Conservation efforts, including reforestation and indigenous land rights, play a crucial role in preserving this vital environment.
