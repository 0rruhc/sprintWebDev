Location and Size

The Amazon Rainforest, the largest tropical rainforest on Earth, spans over 5.5 million square kilometers (2.1 million square miles). It is located in South America and covers parts of nine countries: Brazil, Peru, Colombia, Venezuela, Ecuador, Bolivia, Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana. Brazil holds the largest portion, with about 60% of the rainforest within its borders.

The Amazon River

The Amazon River, the second-longest river in the world, flows through the heart of the rainforest. It stretches over 6,400 kilometers (4,000 miles) and discharges more water than any other river on the planet. The river's vast network of tributaries nourishes the forest, creating an extensive and dynamic ecosystem that supports diverse wildlife and plant species.

Terrain and Landscape

The Amazon Rainforest consists of various terrains, including lowland forests, river basins, and floodplains. The region's landscape is shaped by seasonal flooding and the meandering courses of its rivers. The dense canopy, which reaches up to 50 meters (165 feet) in height, provides habitat for countless species, while the forest floor remains dark and humid.
